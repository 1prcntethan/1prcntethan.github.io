Terminology
Sets x Reps
A set is one group of reps. A rep is one repetition of an exercise. For example: 3 x 5 push-ups means doing 5 push-ups, 3 times (3 sets)
Rest
Usually measured in minutes, rest is the time taken between sets to allow the muscles and body to rest/recover.
Volume
The total amount of exercise performed in one session. High volume means high total amount of reps/holds. Low volume means low total amount of reps/holds.
Holds
Used to refer to static exercises, one hold represents a single attempt of holding a static position.
Failure
The point where you give out and physically cannot complete another rep or hold for another second.
Reps In Reserve (RIR)
The amount of reps before failure; doing a set with 2 RIR means going until you feel like you can do 2 more reps, but stopping instead.
Static Holds/Exercise
Exercises that involve holding a certain position, like planche or front lever. Also known as an isometric exercise. A majority of skills fall under this catergory.
Dynamic Exercise
Exercises that involve movement, like pull-ups or planche leans. Freestyle calisthenics falls under this catergory.
Range Of Motion (ROM)
Refers to the range an exercise goes through, typically for a joint. For example, a full push-up involves a complete range from chest to the floor and back up.
Concentric
The part of an exercise involving the target muscle contracting, muscle length becomes shorter.
Eccentric
The part of an exercise involving the target muscle lengthening, muscle length becomes longer.
Time Under Tension (TUT)
The time during an exercise where the muscle is under tension. This is necessary to provide enough stimulus for muscle growth.
Progression
A harder version/variation of an exercise.
Regression
A easier version/variation of an exercise.
Negatives
Controlled, slow descents during an exercise, focusing on the eccentric phase. For example, slowly lowering yourself during a pull-up.

Skill Difficulty Chart

Baseline

Exercises at this level are accessible for almost everyone, and they serve as a starting point for people starting calisthenics from absolute 0.(No previous athletic experience).

Skills in this catergory: Push-ups, Rows

Low Beginner

Exercises at this level can be achieved within 1-2 months and serve as foundational prerequisites before skill training can begin.

Skills in this catergory: Pullup, Dip, Elbow Lever, L-sit, Frogstand

High Beginner

Exercises at this level can be achieved within 2-6 months and serve as the basic skill progressions of the advanced skills.

Skills in this catergory: Pike PU, Tuck FL, HS

Low Intermediate

Exercises at this level can be achieved within 6-15 months and serve as intermediate skill progressions of the advanced skills.

Skills in this catergory: Tuck PL, HSPU, Adv. Tuck PL, Adv. Tuck FL, MU, V-sit, OAPU

High Intermediate

Exercises at this level can be achieved within 15-36 months and are either the final progressions for the advanced skills, or the final skill itself.

Skills in this catergory: Str. PL, Str. FL, Full FL, 90 deg PU, OAP/OAC, Manna

Advanced

Exercises at this level are usually achieved within 3-5 years and require consistent effort and training to obtain. These are the dream skills of many people.

Skills in this catergory: Full PL, Touch FL, Manna

Elite

Exercises at this level can take up to 5+ years or less depending on genetics and effort. People who can do these skills have truly gone above and beyond.

Skills in this catergory: Dragon PL, Maltese, Full PL PUs, Iron Cross, Inverted Cross
